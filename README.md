# Indian Textile Weaving Master Registry

This registry documents the regional weaving traditions of India, including their workshops, lineages, materials, techniques, motifs, and cultural uniqueness.

## Purpose

To preserve Indian handloom heritage on-chain in a permanent, verifiable, and censorship-resistant format.

Each entry records:

- region  
- lineage or workshop  
- style name  
- materials  
- weaving technique  
- motifs  
- cultural uniqueness  

## Why this registry matters

India has one of the richest textile traditions in the world, including:

- Banarasi Brocade (Uttar Pradesh)  
- Kanchipuram Silk (Tamil Nadu)  
- Patola Double Ikat (Gujarat)  
- Jamdani Muslin (West Bengal)  
- Chanderi Silk-Cotton (Madhya Pradesh)  
- Pochampally Ikat (Telangana)  
- Bhujodi Weaving (Kutch)  
- Kullu Shawls (Himachal Pradesh)  

Each tradition has distinct materials, techniques, motifs, and cultural identity.
